## The 0-4 Severity Scale

- **0 - Not a usability problem** - I don't agree that this is a usability problem at all
- **1 - Cosmetic problem** - Need not be fixed unless extra time is available on project
- **2 - Minor usability problem** - Fixing this should be given low priority
- **3 - Major usability problem** - Important to fix, so should be given high priority
- **4 - Usability catastrophe** - Imperative to fix this before product can be released

## Three Factors for Rating Severity

1. **Frequency** - Is the problem common or rare? How many users will encounter it?
2. **Impact** - Will users be able to easily overcome the problem, or will it seriously impede them?
3. **Persistence** - Is it a one-time problem that users can overcome once they know about it, or will users repeatedly be bothered by the problem?

## When to apply
- Rate every finding in a usability audit or heuristic evaluation
- Use to prioritise fixes - severity 3 and 4 should be addressed before release
- Multiple evaluators should rate independently, then average their ratings
- Combine with heuristic evaluation for structured critique output
- Map to development prioritisation: 4 = blocker, 3 = critical, 2 = normal, 1 = low
