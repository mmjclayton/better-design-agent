## Four Steps

1. **Statements of Meaning** - Responders state what was meaningful, evocative, or striking about the work. Not generic praise but specific observations about what works and why.

2. **Artist as Questioner** - The creator asks questions about the work. This reveals what the creator is uncertain about and directs feedback to where it's most needed.

3. **Neutral Questions** - Responders ask non-judgmental questions. "What was your thinking about the colour of the CTA?" not "Why did you make the CTA green?" Questions seek to understand intent before evaluating execution.

4. **Permissioned Opinions** - Responders ask permission before offering opinions. "I have an opinion about the navigation pattern, would you like to hear it?" This puts the creator in control of what feedback they receive.

## Application to Design Critique

| Step | Design Critique Equivalent |
|------|---------------------------|
| Statements of Meaning | Strengths section - specific observations about what works |
| Artist as Questioner | Context gathering - what is the designer trying to achieve? |
| Neutral Questions | Clarifying intent before critiquing execution |
| Permissioned Opinions | Recommendations section - concrete suggestions |

## When to apply
- Structuring critique output to lead with strengths
- Separating observation from judgment
- Ensuring critique addresses the creator's actual concerns
- Balancing positive and constructive feedback
