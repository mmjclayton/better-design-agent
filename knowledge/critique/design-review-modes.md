## Five Modes (Matched to Design Fidelity)

1. **Inspire** (Strategy/concept stage) - Open exploration. "What if we approached this differently?" Focus on possibilities, not constraints.

2. **Provoke** (Early ideation) - Challenge assumptions. "Does this solve the right problem?" Push the team to defend or reconsider their direction.

3. **Clarify** (Mid-fidelity wireframes) - Seek understanding. "How does a user get from here to there?" Focus on flows, logic, and completeness.

4. **Nudge** (High-fidelity mockups) - Refine execution. "The spacing between these cards is inconsistent." Specific, tactical feedback on implementation.

5. **Nip and Tuck** (Final polish) - Minor adjustments. "This icon is 1px off-centre." Pixel-level precision feedback.

## NNGroup Prerequisites for Useful Critique

1. **Clear scope** - What specifically is being reviewed? The whole page or just the form?
2. **Agreed-upon objectives** - What is this design trying to achieve? Without objectives, critique becomes subjective opinion.
3. **Appropriate fidelity** - Don't give pixel-level feedback on wireframes. Don't give strategy feedback on final builds.

## When to apply
- Match critique depth to design stage (don't critique typography on a wireframe)
- Automated critique operates in modes 4-5 (nudge, nip and tuck) by default
- Adjustable via tone: "opinionated" = nudge, "balanced" = clarify + nudge
- Always state what the design is trying to achieve before evaluating it
