## Research Findings

- **49%** of users operate phones one-handed (thumb only)
- **36%** cradle the phone (one hand holds, other taps)
- **15%** use two hands (two thumbs or index finger)
- **75%** of all interactions are thumb-driven

## Thumb Zone Map

| Zone | Location | Ease of Reach |
|------|----------|---------------|
| Natural | Centre and lower-centre of screen | Easy - place primary actions here |
| Stretch | Upper portion and far edges | Hard - place secondary/infrequent actions here |
| Ow | Top-left corner (right-handed), top-right (left-handed) | Very hard - avoid placing critical actions here |

## Design Implications

1. **Place primary actions at the bottom** - Bottom navigation, floating action buttons, and primary CTAs are easier to reach than top-positioned elements.

2. **Navigation bars at the top are a stretch** - Consider bottom sheets, bottom nav, or swipe gestures for frequent actions.

3. **Avoid top corners for critical actions** - The hamburger menu (top-left) is in the hardest zone for one-handed use.

4. **Full-width buttons at the bottom** - Form submission buttons should span the width at the bottom of the form, within easy thumb reach.

5. **Large enough targets** - 44x44pt minimum, with 48x48dp recommended for primary actions.

## When to apply
- Evaluating mobile or responsive layouts
- Assessing placement of primary CTAs and navigation
- Reviewing form submission button placement
- Checking bottom navigation vs hamburger menu patterns
