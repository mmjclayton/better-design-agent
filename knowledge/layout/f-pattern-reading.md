## Four Reading Patterns

1. **F-pattern** - Users read across the top, then scan down the left side, occasionally reading across. Common on text-heavy pages with weak visual hierarchy. The F-pattern is a symptom of poor design, not a goal.

2. **Layer-cake pattern** - Users scan headings and subheadings, skipping body text. Triggered by clear heading hierarchy. This is the pattern to design for.

3. **Spotted pattern** - Users jump to bold text, links, numbers, and keywords. Design for scanning by bolding key terms and using bulleted lists.

4. **Commitment pattern** - Users read everything. Only occurs with highly motivated users or well-written, relevant content.

## Design Implications

- Place the most important content in the first two paragraphs
- Use descriptive headings and subheadings (layer-cake friendly)
- Start paragraphs and list items with information-carrying words
- Use bold text to highlight key terms (spotted-pattern friendly)
- Left-align text for consistent scan line
- Avoid walls of text - break into scannable chunks

## When to apply
- Content-heavy pages: articles, documentation, settings
- Dashboard layouts: place critical metrics top-left
- Lists and tables: most important columns on the left
- Navigation: primary nav items should be at the top/left of the eye's scan path
