## Eight COGA Design Guidelines

1. **Help users understand what things are and how to use them** - Use familiar design patterns. Make interactive elements look interactive. Use icons with text labels, not icons alone.

2. **Help users find what they need** - Provide clear navigation. Use descriptive headings. Include search. Minimize the number of steps to complete a task.

3. **Use clear content** - Use plain language. Keep sentences short. Define jargon. Use active voice. Chunk information into small, digestible pieces.

4. **Help users avoid mistakes** - Use clear labels. Provide input constraints (date pickers, dropdowns). Show format requirements before input, not after errors. Auto-save work.

5. **Help users focus** - Reduce distractions. Avoid auto-playing media. Minimize interruptions. Group related content together. Use progressive disclosure.

6. **Ensure processes do not rely on memory** - Do not require users to remember information across steps. Show progress indicators. Provide summaries of entered data.

7. **Provide help and support** - Offer contextual help. Provide multiple ways to get assistance. Use tooltips for complex controls. Include examples.

8. **Support adaptation and personalization** - Allow users to customize the interface. Support text resizing. Respect system preferences (reduced motion, contrast).

## When to apply
- Form design: labels, error prevention, format hints
- Multi-step workflows: progress indicators, summaries, undo
- Content-heavy pages: plain language, chunking, headings
- Settings pages: clear descriptions of what each option does
