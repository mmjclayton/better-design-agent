## Core Distinction

- **Inclusive design**: How we design - the tools, methods, and mindset
- **Accessibility**: The outcome - measurable conformance to standards

"Designing for people with permanent disabilities creates solutions that benefit everyone" - the curb-cut effect.

## Three Principles

1. **Recognize exclusion** - Exclusion happens when we solve problems using our own biases. Disability is a mismatch between a person and their environment, not a personal health condition.

2. **Learn from diversity** - People who face the most barriers are experts in navigating those barriers. Include diverse perspectives in the design process.

3. **Solve for one, extend to many** - A solution for a person with a permanent disability often benefits people in situational or temporary contexts. One-handed keyboard use helps someone with an arm injury and someone holding a baby.

## The Persona Spectrum

| Permanent | Temporary | Situational |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| One arm | Arm injury | Holding a baby |
| Blind | Cataracts | Distracted driver |
| Deaf | Ear infection | Loud environment |
| Non-verbal | Laryngitis | Heavy accent |

## When to apply
- When evaluating whether a design accommodates diverse abilities
- When justifying accessibility fixes to stakeholders (the business case)
- When designing input methods, error handling, and feedback mechanisms
- When considering edge cases in user flows
